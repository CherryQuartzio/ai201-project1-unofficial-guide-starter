I was a CompSci major and I lived in Middle Earth my first year. If you don't want to walk a long way to get to class, then you should choose Middle Earth. ME is much closer to the Engineering and Computer Science buildings, which is where a majority of your classes will meet. I also think that most people agree that Middle Earth has better dining commons food than Mesa Court. However, Middle Earth dorm rooms tend to be smaller than the Mesa rooms. They're both good housing options, just depends on your preferences. I don't know much about Mesa Court, but if you have any Middle Earth questions feel free to message me.

Middle:
      Two food commons
      Close to classes
      Lots of studious people
      Lots of study rooms/areas
      Close to library
      Typically characterized as more studious. Plus, people tend to go home on weekends a lot since they live ~1 hr away.
      Mesa:
      Better rooms
      Closer to UTC (University Town center), this place has Chipotle, In N Out, Taco bell, Chick Fil A, Jack in the box, Cha (boba), Gogi (korean version of chipotle), Teriyaki bowl, 24 hr fitness, veggie grill, Le Diplomat (bomb sandwich place), some new sandwich place I haven't been too. But yeah, its relatively close to middle as well, but like across the street from Mesa.
      Mesa is typically stereotyped as partying. I know some people were in halls where they constantly partied. I'm not sure how frequent people go home on weekends compared to Middle. I lived in Middle and it was honestly VERY boring on weekends. Sure you MAY find a kickback somewhere if you try hard enough, but it's a lot easier to find a legit party in Mesa. If I had the chance, I would probably choose Mesa for that partying experience. Middle was fun, but not as crazy. Just know that you have to be on top of your school work and resist a few temptations. If you know you can't back down from a party invite when you have an important exam the next day, choose Middle Earth. You have to do what's best for you and have to know that you have self constraint.
