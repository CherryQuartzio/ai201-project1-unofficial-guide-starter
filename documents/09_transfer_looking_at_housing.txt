ACC would be your best bet. Most transfer students room in ACC anyway. AV on paper sounds nice, but as a nontraditional student, it isn’t conducive due to the maturity difference. ACC also has community engagements and activities, organized by the Community Assistants. Grad and Family housing tends to be more subsidized (cheaper overall rent) with extra perks, but it does not have that much of a community. Mostly lonely and stressed grad students

Transfer student over 25 here. I live in graduate housing. I live in PV graduate housing with 2 PhD student. In terms of community, ACC might be better since it's a jungle over there with graduate and undergrads all packed like sardines in those apartments and rooms. If you're looking for a bigger room for less money definitely do graduate housing. As for "community" at graduate housing, I guess it depends if you get along well with your roommates. Also, if you're planning on bringing a car grad housing offers free parking!
Some graduate told me he's paying like 500 something a month for his own room in VP graduate housing.
